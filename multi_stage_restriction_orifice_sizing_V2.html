Technical Sizing Methodology

The calculation framework executes a multi-variable numerical algorithm based on ISO 5167 guidelines and R.W. Miller's Flow Measurement Engineering Handbook.

  • Equivalent Beta Ratio: Multi-hole orifices are modeled as a single equivalent concentric restriction using $\beta_{eq} = \frac{d \cdot \sqrt{N}}{D}$ where $d$ is individual hole diameter, $N$ is hole count, and $D$ is pipe inside diameter.
  • Acoustic Attenuation: Noise is minimized in multi-hole designs as breaking flow into smaller jet paths shifts acoustic energy to higher frequency octaves, which are absorbed more efficiently by pipe walls (higher transmission loss).
  • Gas Expansion & Iterative Density: For gas service, density and pressure drop are tightly coupled. The solver performs fixed-point iterative loops to compute the gas expansion factor $Y = 1 - (0.41 + 0.35\beta_{eq}^4)\frac{\Delta P}{k P_{\text{in}}}$ alongside variable real-gas density values at each stage.

📋 2. Mathematical Models & Codes Applied

This suite calculates restriction hydraulics utilizing international codes including ISO 5167 and ASME B31.3 2024:

  • Equivalent Beta Ratio ($\beta_{eq}$) Calculates equivalent flow obstruction based on total perforated area across multi-hole plates:
    $$\beta_{eq} = \frac{d \cdot \sqrt{N}}{D}$$
  • ASME B31.3 Plate Thickness ($t$) Determines the safe plate thickness required to avoid structural failure under bending stress at design temperatures:
    $$t \ge D \cdot \sqrt{\frac{3 \cdot \Delta P_{\text{max}}}{16 \cdot S}}$$
    Where $S$ is the allowable material stress dynamically interpolated using ASME Table A-1.
